Preceptor of Data Science at the University of Chicago Data Science Institute, where I develop and implement curricular content for data science and mathematics courses at UChicago and the City Colleges of Chicago. I mentor students through capstone projects (The Clinic), Data Science for Social Impact collaborations, and applied industry initiatives, and I serve on the graduate admissions committee.

My research sits at the intersection of probabilistic numerical methods, machine learning, and education. I hold a Ph.D. in Applied Mathematics from the University of North Carolina at Charlotte — dissertation: *Probabilistic Numerical Integration with Applications in Machine Learning* — and a Data Science and Machine Learning Certificate from MIT.

---

### Teaching

My teaching philosophy is grounded in inclusivity, practical relevance, collaboration, growth, and flexibility. Having followed a non-traditional path to academia myself, I am committed to creating learning environments where students of varied backgrounds and preparation levels can engage meaningfully and succeed. I emphasize active learning — students manipulate data, debug code, and work through problems themselves rather than passively observing — and I prioritize real, contemporary datasets that connect to students' lived experiences.

I have taught across a wide range of institutions and levels: K–12, introductory, and upper-level data science at UChicago; introductory and advanced mathematics and statistics at the City Colleges of Chicago, Belmont Abbey College, and UNC Charlotte. Each setting has shaped how I adapt instruction while maintaining disciplinary rigor.

### Service

Strong departments and institutions are built through collective effort. My service strengths lie in student mentorship and admissions — both rooted in my own undergraduate experience, where faculty guidance was transformative.

I have mentored students in NSF-funded undergraduate research, The Clinic (senior capstone), and the Data Science for Social Impact program at UChicago. I have served on hiring, admissions, strategic planning, and promotion and tenure committees across institutions, and served as Chair of the Department of Mathematics and Physics at Belmont Abbey College. I have been an NSF panelist (2023, 2024) and served on program committees for AIED, PME-NA, and LOD.

I am a member of the Board of Directors of Accessible Math (501(c)(3)) and a member of Pi Mu Epsilon, the American Mathematical Society, and the Mathematical Association of America.
