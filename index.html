Curriculum Vitae

Biography

My path to academia was not linear. After attending several undergraduate institutions and stepping away from higher education for a period, I ultimately completed my degree at a university where faculty were approachable, cultivated curiosity, and recognized my potential before I did. Their mentorship changed the trajectory of my life, and it is the model I carry into every classroom.

I am currently a Preceptor of Data Science at the University of Chicago Data Science Institute, where I develop and implement curricular content for data science and mathematics courses at UChicago and the City Colleges of Chicago. I mentor students through capstone projects (The Clinic), Data Science for Social Impact, Data4All and applied industry initiatives.

I hold a Ph.D. in Applied Mathematics from the University of North Carolina at Charlotte (dissertation: Probabilistic Numerical Integration with Applications in Machine Learning) and a Data Science and Machine Learning Certificate from MIT.

Teaching Philosophy

My teaching philosophy is grounded in inclusivity, practical relevance, collaboration, and growth. I have built courses and curricula across a wide range of institutional contexts — a private R1 (University of Chicago), a community college system (City Colleges of Chicago), a liberal arts college (Belmont Abbey College), and a large public R1 (University of North Carolina at Charlotte).

Data science and mathematics are disciplines learned by doing. I model logical reasoning aloud, including the dead ends and moments of uncertainty, and progressively transfer explanatory responsibility to students. My goal is not just technical competence; it is analytical agency: the confidence to approach an unfamiliar problem, break it down, and reason through it rigorously. See the Teaching page for my full course history.

Research Interests

My research spans probabilistic numerical methods, machine learning, and mathematics and data science education. I collaborate with Outlier Research & Evaluation in their efforts to explore the spread of data science. I have published in AI in Education, machine learning, and mathematics education venues.
